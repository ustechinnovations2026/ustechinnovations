Acid Value and Viscosity Tracking in Alkyd Resin Cooking

Predict reaction endpoints and acid values continuously in high-temperature chemical reactors.

The Risk of Gelling in Resin Polymerization

Alkyd resin manufacturing relies on esterification cooking at temperatures up to 240—C. Reaction progress is tracked by Acid Value (AV) and viscosity. If AV drops too low, the batch polymerizes completely and gelation occurs, ruining the reactor. Manual sampling is slow and dangerous.

High-Temperature Process Probes

USTECH high-temperature sapphire immersion probes are installed directly inside the resin reactor wall. The probes withstand continuous exposure to hot, viscous mixtures. Live AV and viscosity predictions are updated continuously, guiding the exact dilution endpoint.

References

  • "In-situ Monitoring of Poly-Esterification Acid Value and Viscosity under High Temperatures," Industrial & Engineering Chemistry Research, 2024.
