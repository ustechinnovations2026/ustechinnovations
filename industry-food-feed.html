The Challenges You Face

Feed and food manufacturers operate in an environment of thin margins, volatile commodity prices, and intensifying regulatory pressure. Every percentage point of protein or moisture has a direct impact on profitability, animal performance, and label compliance. Yet traditional laboratory workflows create a fundamental problem: by the time results arrive, the batch is already produced.

01

Rising Input Costs & Formulation Waste

Ingredient nutritional profiles deviate continuously. Lacking real-time analysis, manufacturers over-formulate high-cost constituents (e.g., soybean meal) as a safety buffeR²incurring over $150,000 in annual raw material giveaways.

02

Delayed Quality Decisions

Conventional wet chemistry assays require 2—4 hours. During this latency window, mills operate blindly; any composition deviations force costly batch reworks, dilution blending, or complete product disposal.

03

Regulatory & Labeling Exposure

Compliance frameworks (e.g., EU 767/2009, FDA 21 CFR 501) mandate declared nutritional profiles remain within tight tolerance bands. Off-spec shipments trigger client disputes, severe audit exposure, and steep contract penalties.

02 — Formulation & Mixing Optimization

Formulate to Target, Not to Worst-Case Assumptions.

Traditional formulation relies on database averages or supplier certificates — both of which lag behind the actual quality of ingredients on your production floor today. By scanning each ingredient batch before it enters the mixer, your formulation software receives real-time nutritional profiles, enabling dynamic least-cost recipe adjustments.

The result: you formulate closer to nutritional targets without safety margins built on uncertainty. For a typical 50 ton/hour feed mill, eliminating just 0.5% protein over-formulation translates to annual savings exceeding $120,000 in soybean meal costs alone.

03 — Process Control & Conditioning

Close the Loop Between Measurement and Action.

Mount inline NIR sensors at the mixer discharge or above the conditioning drum to continuously monitor feed composition in real time. When moisture deviates from the conditioning target — due to seasonal grain variability or ambient humidity changes — the system communicates directly with your PLC to adjust steam injection or water dosing rates automatically.

This closed-loop control eliminates the guesswork from conditioning. Operators no longer rely on visual inspection or hourly grab samples. The result is consistent pellet durability, reduced energy consumption at the pellet die, and fewer fines in finished product.
