Crude Fiber and Ash Content Determination in Feed Rations using FT-NIR

Continuous monitoring of crude fiber, total ash, and moisture parameters in compound feed mills using at-line spectroscopy.

Compound feed mills in the food and feed industry blend a diverse mix of grains, meals, and co-products to formulate species-specific feed rations. For these operations, crude fiber (CF) and mineral (total ash) fractions are subject to strict regulatory labeling rules. Furthermore, fiber content determines digestion performance and gut health. Traditional crude fiber testing (acid/base extraction) takes hours, and ash verification requires heating ovens to 550—C, meaning mill operators usually get results a day late.

"MEMS-based FT-NIR technology measures moisture, protein, fat, fiber, and ash in animal feed simultaneously, giving operators actionable data in 60 seconds."

Analytical Methodology and Chemometrics

Spectroscopic assessment of fiber and ash parameters relies on the absorption behaviors of organic matrices. Although minerals (ash) do not absorb NIR light directly, they exhibit strong correlations with hydrogen-bonded salts and hydration bands in organic components. Our USTECH MasterLine at-line features an automated sample cup to deliver high-quality scans of finished feeds and raw meals.

Collected spectra are preprocessed inside `caliX` with scatter correction (SNV) and 1st derivative filters to eliminate particle-size variance. The PLS regression models then correlate this processed spectral data to reference lab values. True model portability is maintained thanks to our highly stable silicon-MEMS chip interferometer alignment.

FT-NIR Feed Calibration Accuracy Metrics

The table below summarizes calibration performance and validation accuracy (RMSEP) compared to standard ISO wet chemistry references:

Key B2B Value Additions

Real-time feed analysis supports mill efficiency in several key areas:

  • Formula Optimization: Limits over-addition of expensive protein or fat ingredients, lowering the overall cost of feed formulations.
  • Co-product Monitoring: Tracks highly variable agricultural co-products (e.g. wheat bran or DDGS) as they arrive, adjusting formulations to keep fiber within spec.
  • Energy Savings: Reduces muffle furnace heating runs in the laboratory, cutting daily operational energy consumption.

Conclusion

USTECH Innovations feed analyzers allow compound feed mills to replace legacy batch testing with instant, multi-parameter validation, maximizing profit margin and product quality.
