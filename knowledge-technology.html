NIR Technology & Calibration Science

Understand how near-infrared spectroscopy, advanced calibration models, and our integrated software ecosystem deliver real-time process intelligence.

The Science Behind NIR Spectroscopy

Near-Infrared (NIR) spectroscopy measures the light absorption of molecular chemical bonds (primarily C-H, O-H, and N-H). By projecting broad-spectrum NIR light onto a sample, the analyzer measures which wavelengths are absorbed and which are reflected back. This absorption pattern creates a unique spectral fingerprint that reveals the chemical composition of the material.

USTECH leverages FT-NIR (Fourier Transform Near-Infrared) technology in its analyzer portfolio, delivering high spectral resolution across a wide wavelength range (1,350–2,550 nm). Combined with our proprietary calibration models and software ecosystem, this technology enables real-time, non-destructive composition analysis directly on the production line — eliminating the delays and costs of traditional laboratory methods.

How It Works

From Light to Data: The Measurement Path

Trace the pathway from NIR light emission through spectral analysis to actionable process data — powered by USTECH calibration models and software.

01

Broadband Emission

A stabilized, long-lifetime halogen light source projects a continuous spectrum of near-infrared light onto the sample material.

02

Selective Absorption

Organic molecules in the sample (C-H, O-H, N-H bonds) absorb specific wavelengths depending on their concentration, reflecting the rest.

03

FT-NIR Spectral Engine

The reflected light enters the FT-NIR interferometer inside the analyzer, which separates the wavelengths and generates a high-resolution spectral interferogram.

04

caliX Calibration Models

The raw spectrum is processed through USTECH's caliX calibration models, which use advanced chemometric algorithms (PLS, AutoML) to predict chemical composition in milliseconds.

05

ProChem Process Output

Predicted values (protein, moisture, fat, etc.) are transmitted via ProChem to your PLC/SCADA system through OPC UA, Modbus, or analog outputs for real-time process control.

Software / Chemometrics

caliX — Intelligent Calibration Suite

caliX is USTECH's proprietary chemometrics and calibration software that transforms raw spectral data into actionable chemical composition values. Designed for both expert spectroscopists and first-time users, it automates the entire calibration lifecycle.

What is caliX?

caliX is a desktop chemometrics application that manages the full pipeline from spectral data import through preprocessing, outlier detection, model building, and validation — all within a single unified workspace. It bridges the gap between laboratory reference analysis and real-time production measurement.

Unlike generic statistics software, caliX is purpose-built for NIR spectroscopy workflows. It understands spectral data natively, offering specialized tools like Savitzky-Golay smoothing, multiplicative scatter correction (MSC), standard normal variate (SNV), and first/second derivative preprocessing — all accessible through a visual, click-driven interface.

caliX supports all USTECH hardware platforms (ProLine2550, ProLine17ES, USTECH MasterLine, USTECH StarchQC, USTECH SamplePrep, USTECH Tornado+) and manages calibration model deployment across your instrument fleet from a centralized workspace.

Core Capabilities

AutoML Engine

Automated model building that tests hundreds of preprocessing & regression combinations, ranking results by cross-validation performance (RMSECV, R², RPD).

Advanced Outlier Detection

Mahalanobis distance, leverage/residual analysis, Hotelling's T², and spectral residual methods to automatically flag and isolate anomalous samples.

Spectral Preprocessing

MSC, SNV, Savitzky-Golay derivatives (1st/2nd), Norris-Williams smoothing, detrending, and wavelength region selection (iPLS) — all visually configurable.

Regression Algorithms

Partial Least Squares (PLS), Principal Component Regression (PCR), Multiple Linear Regression (MLR), and Support Vector Regression (SVR).

Classification Models

PCA-based classification, SIMCA, and discriminant analysis for raw material identification and authentication applications.

Calibration Transfer

One-click model deployment across all units. USTECH's high unit-to-unit instrument similarity means zero slope/bias correction is needed.

caliX Calibration Workflow

01

Data Import

Import spectral scans (.xlsx, .csv) paired with laboratory reference values. caliX auto-detects wavelength columns and constituent parameters.

02

Data Cleaning

Identify and remove outliers using multivariate statistical methods. Thin redundant samples to build a balanced, representative calibration set.

03

Preprocessing

Apply spectral transformations (MSC, SNV, derivatives) to remove scatter effects and enhance chemical signal clarity.

04

Model Building

Use AutoML or manual regression to build and optimize calibration models. Cross-validation ensures robustness against overfitting.

05

Deployment

Export validated models directly to ProChem for real-time inline prediction. Models can be pushed to all connected instruments simultaneously.

Software / Process Automation

ProChem — Real-Time Process Integration Platform

ProChem is USTECH's industrial-grade process automation software that bridges NIR spectroscopy measurements with plant control systems. It is the operational backbone that transforms laboratory-calibrated models into live, actionable process data.

What is ProChem?

ProChem is the edge-computing software platform that runs directly at the production line level. It receives calibration models built in caliX and applies them in real-time to incoming spectral scans from USTECH analyzers. The predicted chemical composition values are then communicated to factory PLC/SCADA systems through industrial communication protocols.

ProChem is designed for 24/7 unattended operation in demanding industrial environments. It manages instrument health monitoring, automatic reference scanning, lamp failure detection with dual-lamp switchover, and spectral quality validation — all without requiring operator intervention.

Whether your production facility uses Siemens, Allen-Bradley, Schneider, or ABB automation infrastructure, ProChem communicates natively through industry-standard protocols (OPC UA, Modbus TCP/RTU, 4–20 mA analog, REST API) to deliver measurement data exactly where your control logic needs it.

ProChem Core Features

Multi-Analyzer Management

Connect and manage multiple USTECH analyzers from a single ProChem instance. Monitor all measurement points across your facility from one dashboard.

Real-Time Trending & Visualization

Live trend charts for all measured constituents with configurable alarm limits, moving averages, and SPC (Statistical Process Control) overlays.

Automatic Health Monitoring

Continuous self-diagnostics including lamp intensity tracking, reference scan validation, spectral noise monitoring, and automatic dual-lamp failover.

Batch & Continuous Mode

Configure measurement schedules for batch processes (blenders, reactors) or continuous flow (conveyors, pipelines) with adjustable scan averaging.

User Access Control

Multi-level user management (Operator, Supervisor, Admin) with audit logging. 21 CFR Part 11 compliant electronic records for pharmaceutical applications.

Remote Access & Monitoring

Secure remote dashboard access via web browser. Monitor instrument health, review trends, and receive alarm notifications from anywhere.

Typical Use Case

A feed mill installs a ProLine2550 on the mixer discharge conveyor. ProChem receives spectral scans every 3 seconds, predicts protein, moisture, and fat using caliX-built models, and sends the values via OPC UA to the Siemens S7-1500 PLC. The PLC automatically adjusts raw material dosing gates to keep protein within ±0.15% of the target specification — eliminating expensive protein give-away and preventing out-of-spec batches.
